Modus Operandi for Contracted Jobs

Information Technology and Communication, ITC

The role of technology is to make operations easier and to accelerate operations and consequently produce prompt results. Technology also improves the capacities and capabilities of the human resource deployed
A majority of DIL’s business and activities are carried out online. The entire recruitment process except for the interviews is done online. It then follows that the greatest investment has gone into developing a robust website and database infrastructure.

Data on applicants and job orders is collected responsibly and intelligently. The software program was developed for the entry of data from financial transactions as well as for the generation of reports.

Communication

Emails, phone systems and intranets set up for efficient communication within the company are seamlessly and efficiently linked.

Data Mining Tools

Modus Operandi for Contracted Jobs

Depending on the tasks to be accomplished, DIL has a dedicated team of professionals embedded to the Client Project Manager for efficient execution of tasks at hand and advance planning. The Company coordinates and activates all the relevant cells within their data base for immediate deployment if need be within 24 hour notice and handles all the logistics pertaining to following:
  1. Transportation of the workers to and from place of abode.
  2. Arrange for their meals and accommodation.
  3. Pay for their Medical Insurance and Statutory Government deductions.
  4. Provide all the safety gear and tools while at work or make arrangement with the client for such provisions at a cost.
  5. Ensure all HSE protocols are adhered to as per Public Health Regulations
  6. Enforce and ensure discipline and good work ethics are maintained at all times

Disaster preparedness & Emergency Management Courses

INTRODUCTION:

DIMACS International Ltd was formed by a consortium of experts from diversified fields in Disaster Management. The experts are mostly National and International retired Military Personnel who have had first-hand experience in dealing with real disasters worldwide. Others are highly experienced and respected academia-support-group who have been authority lecturers on the subject in some of the finest universities in the world for several years. We are also proud to be an institution affiliated in partnership with Kenyatta University. That means all the course materials and delivery methods are vetted and certified by the University. We award certificates and diplomas up to Degree level, in conjunction with the University depending on the course offered. Disasters can occur spontaneously without aforethought or can build up quietly without anyone taking notice. They can be natural or manmade. Whatever the case, there is a need for mitigation, preparedness, response and eventual recovery from the catastrophe. In the year 2013 alone, Kenya has witnessed a number of disasters ranging from Fires at the JKIA, grisly road accidents, PSVs blown up by terrorists, collapsing buildings, terrorist attack on Westgate Mall, floods etc. just to name but a few. Currently the worst disaster creeping into our own society and ruining the lives of many young people and the old as well as ALCOHOL and DRUG abuse. Unless, we focus and deliberately confront these new disaster phenomena, then we will loose a whole generation as a nation in terms of unproductive, inebriated population. All these disasters affected and will continue to affect the lives and livelihoods of millions of Kenyans causing untold suffering both to the individuals and the Nation State as a whole. Some of the courses are tailored to target senior management team in core areas who can directly influence the outcome of desired actions to mitigate disasters in their Areas of Responsibility (AOR). The following topics are covered in depth including practical demonstrations if need be.

MODULE 1:- ALCOHOL AND DRUG ABUSE AS A NATIONAL DISASTER

DIMACS International has partnered with Kenyatta University- Regional Centre for Capacity Development, KU-RCD to sensitize both the Youth and the general populace at large on the adverse effects on over-indulgence of the subject. KU-RCD will utilize her extensive countrywide network coverage to reach out to the General Populace and specifically the youth through workshops, seminars, counseling, rehabilitation and finally establishing ToTs working groups at County Levels and if need be at the Ward Levels. This will ensure continuity and ease of monitoring impact assessment on the ground. For the success of this project, DIMACS International is further partnering with the following groups to mobilize the target audience.
  1. Organized Youth groups
  2. Schools
  3. Churches
